Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

For Gulab Jamun Balls:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter), melted
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • Oil or ghee for frying

For Sugar Syrup (Chashni):

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

For Frying:

  • Ghee or oil for deep frying

Cooking Instructions

Prepare Dough: In a mixing bowl, crumble the khoya. Add all-purpose flour, baking soda, and ghee to the crumbled khoya. Knead the mixture into a smooth and soft dough. If it's too dry, add a little milk as needed.

Shape into Balls: Divide the dough into small, smooth balls. Ensure there are no cracks on the surface.

Heat Ghee or Oil: Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.

Fry the Balls: Once the ghee or oil is hot, reduce the heat to low-medium. Carefully add the prepared gulab jamun balls to the hot ghee. Fry them until they turn golden brown, turning them gently for even cooking. Remove and place them on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.

Prepare Sugar Syrup: In a separate pan, combine sugar and water to make a sugar syrup. Bring it to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely. Add cardamom powder, saffron strands (if using), and rose water. Simmer the syrup for 5-7 minutes until it attains a slightly sticky consistency.

Soak in Sugar Syrup: Drop the fried gulab jamun balls into the warm sugar syrup. Let them soak for at least 2-3 hours to absorb the sweetness.

Serve: Once soaked, Gulab Jamun is ready to be served.
