Rasamalai

Ingredients

For Paneer Dumplings:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • A pinch of baking soda (optional)

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • A few saffron strands
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

For Rabri (Reduced Milk):

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • A few saffron strands
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts for garnish (almonds, pistachios)

Cooking Instructions

Prepare Paneer: Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle the milk. Once the curds separate, strain them through a muslin cloth. Rinse the paneer under cold water to remove any trace of lemon or vinegar. Knead the paneer until it becomes smooth and soft.

Shape and Cook Dumplings: Add all-purpose flour and baking soda (if using) to the paneer. Knead again. Divide the mixture into small, equal-sized portions and shape them into flat discs. In a separate pan, bring water to a boil. Add a little sugar. Gently drop the paneer discs into the boiling water and cook for about 10-12 minutes. Once cooked, remove them and place in cold water.

Prepare Sugar Syrup: In another pot, combine sugar and water to make a sugar syrup. Add saffron strands and cardamom powder. Bring the syrup to a boil, ensuring the sugar dissolves completely. Allow it to cool slightly.

Soak Paneer Dumplings: Gently squeeze out the excess water from the cooked paneer dumplings. Place them in the warm sugar syrup and let them soak for at least 2-3 hours.

Prepare Rabri: Boil milk in a wide, heavy-bottomed pan. Add sugar, saffron strands, and cardamom powder. Simmer on low heat, stirring frequently until the milk reduces to half its original volume.

Assemble Rasamalai: Take a serving dish and place the soaked paneer dumplings in it. Pour the reduced milk (rabri) over the dumplings. Garnish with chopped nuts.

Chill and Serve: Refrigerate the Rasamalai for a few hours before serving. Serve chilled and enjoy the creamy goodness!
