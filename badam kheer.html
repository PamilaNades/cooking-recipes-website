Badam Kheer

Ingredients

  • 1 cup almonds, soaked overnight and peeled
  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped almonds and pistachios for garnish

Cooking Instructions

Prepare Almond Paste: After soaking the almonds overnight, peel them. Grind the peeled almonds into a smooth paste using a little milk. Set aside.

Cook Rice: Soak rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain the water and grind the rice into a coarse paste using a little milk.

Boil Milk: In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the whole milk to a boil. Add the ground rice paste, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.

Add Almond Paste: Once the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens, add the almond paste and continue to stir.

Sweeten and Flavor: Add sugar and mix well until it dissolves. Add cardamom powder for flavor.

Optional Saffron Infusion: If using saffron, soak a pinch of saffron strands in a tablespoon of warm milk for a few minutes. Add this to the kheer for a beautiful color and aroma.

Simmer: Let the kheer simmer on low heat until it reaches your desired consistency.

Garnish and Serve: Garnish the Badam Kheer with chopped almonds and pistachios. Serve it warm or chilled, depending on your preference.
